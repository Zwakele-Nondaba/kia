What is a Matric Rewrite?
A Matric rewrite allows learners to re-write specific Grade 12 subjects to improve their final NSC results without repeating the entire year.
Who is eligible for a Matric Rewrite?
Anyone who has previously written the NSC examinations and wants to improve their marks or meet entry requirements for further study or employment.
Is a Matric Rewrite recognised by universities and employers?
Yes. Rewritten results are issued by the Department of Basic Education and are officially recognised by universities, colleges, and employers.
What documents are required to register?
Copy of your ID
Copy of your original Matric statement or certificate
Parent/guardian ID (if under 18)
Is the registration fee refundable?
No, the registration fee is non-refundable.
Can I register if I failed Matric completely?
Yes. As long as you wrote the NSC exams previously, you are eligible to rewrite selected subjects.
Will my old marks be replaced?
Only improved marks replace previous results. If your rewrite mark is lower, your original mark remains.
Will I receive a new Matric certificate?
Yes. Once results are released, a new statement of results is issued, and your certificate is updated if applicable.
